What is Risograph/Lisograph?

Risograph printing is a special kind of printing that was developed in Japan. The process is similar to screen printing, where different layers/stencils are used to add one color at at time. It is sometimes called "digital screen printing". It is a cheaper and faster than the printers you are probably used to, and is often used to print large abounts of work, like posters, prints, and zines. They are famous for having vivid colors and a stamp-like quality.
Lisograph is just a play on my name (Lisa). I do not have a Risograph printer, but I thought it would be cool to imitate the process digitally, by creating a drawing digitally, then creating masks out of the layers, which I then add colors from a colorscheme generating API called Colormind. It was a wild ride figuring out how to color png images and properly aligning all the layers, but I hope you enjoy it!

-Lisa
